furaha cooperative for both present and future investments

Furaha cooperative sacco is a Savings and Credit Cooperative Organization (SACCO) is a member-driven financial institution designed to promote savings and provide affordable credit to its members. SACCOs operate on a cooperative model, meaning all members have an equal say in its operations and share in its success.

Joining a SACCO offers numerous advantages. Members gain access to affordable credit facilities, enabling them to invest in businesses, pay for education, or meet other financial goals without the burden of high-interest rates. The shared savings model also ensures members can benefit from dividends based on the SACCO’s profitability, making it a sustainable and rewarding financial option.

Annual Sacco Society Levy 2024

Introduction

Data from the State Department of Cooperatives shows that SACCO savings’ value hit one trillion mark in the financial year 2023, for the first time amid economic challenges. While this is a huge milestone for the SACCO movement, the Government has introduced a new levy on the non- withdrawable SACCO deposits. Non- withdrawable SACCO deposits mean the share capital paid by a SACCO member that cannot be reduced or removed. Further, a SACCO is categorized as non-withdrawable deposit taking if it operates in receiving deposits that are not available for withdrawal for the duration of membership but can be used as collateral to obtain loans .

Tax Rate and Deduction

A schedule by the Sacco Societies Regulatory Authority (SASRA) shows that all SACCOs registered under the Sacco Societies (Non-Deposit Taking Business) Regulations, 2020, would from January 1, 2024; pay a varied annual levy over the next four years up to December 31, 2027. The levy will be known as Annual Sacco Societies Levy and it will be set at 0.10% of the non-withdrawable deposits for 2024, before climbing to 0.13% in 2025, 0.14% in 2026, and 0.15% in 2027. Specifically, the levy paid shall be based on the total non-withdrawable deposits held by the Sacco society as indicated by the audited financial statements of the Sacco society for the immediately preceding financial year.

The Purpose of the Levy

While in the recent past, the government has put a lot of effort into boosting revenues through tax collection, the main aim of deducting the levy is to fund the sector regulator’s operations (SASRA). In addition, the levy will aid SASRA to gain some financial independence by reducing its reliability on the Exchequer

Share capital refers to the funds raised by a company or cooperative (like a SACCO) through the issuance of shares to its members or investors. In the context of a SACCO, share capital represents the money contributed by members as part of their ownership stake in the cooperative. These shares are typically purchased by members at a specified price, and the total share capital is used to fund the SACCO’s operations, such as providing loans and other financial services to its members. The number of shares held by a member often determines their voting power and dividend entitlement, as well as their eligibility to access certain services or loans within the SACCO. Share capital is typically seen as a long-term investment, and it can grow over time as the SACCO becomes more successful.
