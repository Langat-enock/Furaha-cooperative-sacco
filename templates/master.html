    #} {# #} {##} {# What is Share Capital?#} {# #} {#

    #} {# #} {#
    #} {# Share capital refers to the funds raised by a company or cooperative (like a SACCO) through the#} {# issuance of shares to its members or investors. In the context of a SACCO, share capital#} {# represents the money contributed by members as part of their ownership stake in the cooperative.#} {# These shares are typically purchased by members at a specified price, and the total share#} {# capital is used to fund the SACCOâ€™s operations, such as providing loans and other financial#} {# services to its members.#} {##} {# The number of shares held by a member often determines their voting power and dividend#} {# entitlement, as well as their eligibility to access certain services or loans within the SACCO.#} {# Share capital is typically seen as a long-term investment, and it can grow over time as the#} {# SACCO becomes more successful.#} {##} {##} {#
